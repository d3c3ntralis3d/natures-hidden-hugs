The Story

Puddles was the smallest duckling in the pond, often pushed aside by his siblings. When Sarah found him alone on the bank, shivering and unable to keep up, she knew he needed help. She brought him home, intending to release him once he was strong enough.

But Puddles had other plans. He imprinted on Sarah immediately, following her everywhere‚Äîfrom the garden to the grocery store (in a special bag!). He became more like a puppy than a duck, greeting her with excited quacks whenever she walked in the room.

Their bond defied expectations. Puddles would sit on her lap while she read, swim alongside her in the lake, and even got along with the family dog. It showed that love and connection can come in the most unexpected forms, transcending species boundaries.

"He didn't know he was a duck, and I didn't care. We were just family."
